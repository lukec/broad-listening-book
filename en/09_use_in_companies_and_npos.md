# Chapter 9: Applications in Companies and Nonprofits

Broad listening is not a technique only for politics or government. The need to "hear the voices of many people and grasp the bigger picture" is very much present in companies and nonprofits as well.

In this chapter, we introduce case studies from Altius Link, which operates contact center services, and Cybozu, which provides groupware. Both companies adopted broad listening methods to better understand the voices of customers and employees and to apply those insights to operational improvement and organizational management.

Altius Link is a BPO company jointly funded by KDDI and Mitsui & Co., and it operates one of the largest contact center networks in Japan. Vast numbers of customer comments and inquiries flow into these contact centers every day, but there were limited ways to understand the overall picture. Broad listening was introduced as a method for drawing deeper insights from the accumulated data and capturing customer voices more fully.

Cybozu is a software company that provides groupware products such as kintone and Cybozu Office. For a company whose business is fundamentally about supporting information sharing and consensus-building within organizations, the question of how to make diverse internal opinions visible and connect them to decision-making is also a challenge within its own organization. Broad listening was introduced as a way to facilitate the flow of voices within the organization.
