# Chapter 8: Use in Local Governments

Broad listening technology is being used not only in elections, but also in the everyday settings where local governments engage residents in civic participation.

In Ota City, Gunma Prefecture, the AI-assisted tool "Baisoku Kaigi" ("Double-Speed Meetings"), developed by Polyphonic Reality (see Chapter 11), was introduced to the "Jibungotoka Kaigi" ("Making It Personal" Conference) organized by Koso Nippon. This is an example of how the tool helped bridge gaps in background knowledge among randomly selected residents and significantly improved the quality of discussion.

In Hiroshima Prefecture, as part of the work of the Hiroshima AI Lab, an AI exploration hub established within the prefectural government, a broad listening pilot was conducted using a public comment analysis AI developed by DD2030. This case focused on analyzing approximately 3,000 open-ended responses submitted by residents and on the coordination process needed for local governments to put AI-generated output to practical use.
